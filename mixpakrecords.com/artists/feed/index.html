He rolls off a few formative experiences: Richie Hawtin playing live in a warehouse completely covered in black plastic, people doing the worm […]]]> Born in St.Louis, Mr. One Hundred’s early dance music education consisted of seriously underage raving at a time when the American Midwest had arguably the best underground party scene in the world. He rolls off a few formative experiences: Richie Hawtin playing live in a warehouse completely covered in black plastic, people doing the worm to a 6am Moodyman set in an abandoned St. Louis strip mall storefront, and Robert Armani DJing an entire set wearing white gloves. He co-founded a label and made deeper connections to Detroit by travelling to the motor city for all the production – every vinyl was mastered by Ron Murphy (the same guy who mastered Kevin Saunderson, Underground Resistance, +8, Derrick May and Jeff Mills). After a tour of duty through Chicago’s art & performance scenes, Mr. One Hundred ended up in London, UK, and was deeply influened by London’s cultural and musical milieu.

Then two things then happened. As Mr. One Hundred explains it, “First I started DJing at the (now legendary) 7 Year Glitch and Teens of Thailand parties where I met Silverlink – he was the first DJ I saw mix soca into Jeff Mills and it blew my mind. Second is I lived next to a Trinidadian-run BBQ chicken shop whose owner Lyndon was an ex-Soca DJ, and he started feeding me mixtapes of his old crew Spoil Bratz. Plus I was going out in London, hearing soca mixed up with other kinds of music, going to carnival, all of that. I decided to experiment with soca rhythms, fusing them with detroit techno sounds to make a new type of music. I tried my first beat, H.O.W. Riddim, and when I played it for Lyndon he just said “Yep, that’s it” and I went from there. Now with the Top Nice crew we’ve created an audience for this new soca sound at our parties, H.O.W. Riddim is on the Mixpak release, and I have a name for what I make: Power Soca music from the future!!!”

Palmistry is the project of British musician Benjy Keating. A vocalist and producer with a singular vision and voice, Palmistry makes hybridized pop music that is both distinctly new and yet, like the best songwriting, sounds instantly familiar.
His debut album for Mixpak, Pagan, was hailed as “an emotional blend of recognizable and alien pop music” by The New Yorker and “an earnest reflection on London’s lost youth” by Crack Magazine. His natural talent at pop craftsmanship was confirmed when his song “Lifted” was interpolated by Cashmere Cat, Major Lazer, and Tory Lanez for their song “Miss You”.
Palmistry released his sophomore LP Afterlife in 2019.

London via Midlands DJ and producer Murlo is a unique presence on the UK club scene. Moving effortlessly and fearlessly through the landscape of grime, dancehall, funky, garage and bassline, his sound is at once recognizable. His intricate melodies, playful sampling and consistent emphasis on bass make for an ideal club aesthetic – boundary-pushing music with a dependable dancefloor hype.

Influenced as much by the sonic narratives of film scores as the recent history of UK dance music, Murlo is a storyteller pinning together weird sounds and club mutations, never forgetting the Caribbean and African roots of London’s scene.

With a string of successful EPs and much sought after edits, his impressive quantity / quality ratio has gained him respect and support from dedicated fans, tastemakers, press and promoters around the world.

[…]]]> Born and raised in Lyon, France, Douster started his musical life somewhere between the popular sound of eurodance and the 50s rhythm n blues tapes that were blasted from his family car. At the age of just 8, he turned to French rap and hip hop music would stay with him during his teenage years.

After buying his first MKII’s at the age 14 to start scratching, he very quickly moved into production and started making beats for his friends. Under the influence of dancehall music and high powered dub soundsystems, he started to add heavy sub bass to his productions. And when a close friend brought him a crate full of smiley faced vinyl, he fell in love with acid house and early 90s New Beat.

With releases on top labels such as Mad Decent or Sound Pellegrino and widely acclaimed remixes for the likes of Crookers, Rusko, Yolanda Be Cool and Kid Sister, his sound has quickly gained international notoriety.
